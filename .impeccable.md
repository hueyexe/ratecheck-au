## Design Context

### App
**RateCheck** — free, open-source Australian mortgage rate comparison tool. No ads, no affiliate links, no commercial bias. Live at https://hueyexe.github.io/aus-mortgage-comparator/

### Users
**Primary: everyday Australian homebuyer.** Non-technical, time-poor, financially motivated. They're comparing rates before calling a broker or bank — they want a fast, honest answer to "who has the best rate for my situation?" They are not financial professionals. Copy must be plain language. Data must be scannable, not overwhelming.

Secondary: mortgage brokers and refinancers who want density, filters, CSV export, and analytics. Progressive disclosure bridges both audiences — simple surface, depth on demand.

### Brand Personality
**Approachable, honest, data-forward.** Three words: **clear, trustworthy, fresh.**

This is a community tool, not a corporate product. The personality is warm and direct — like a knowledgeable friend who happens to know a lot about mortgages. Not a bank. Not a fintech startup trying to look slick. Not a government portal.

Emotional goal: users should feel **confident and informed**, not overwhelmed or sold to.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Friendly but data-dense.** Inspired by Up Bank's approach — personality and warmth in the chrome, serious information in the content. Not a copy of Up Bank. Not Bloomberg Terminal. The sweet spot between the two.

**Colour system:**
- Accent: warm teal `oklch(0.60 0.18 175)` — trustworthy, fresh, distinctly not corporate indigo
- Neutrals: warm sand `oklch(0.98–0.10 0.01–0.02 80)` — subtle warmth, not cold grey
- Rate down (good): `oklch(0.60 0.17 155)` — green
- Rate up (bad): `oklch(0.58 0.20 25)` — rose/red
- Sky blue for fixed rates, amber for warnings

**Typography:**
- DM Sans — friendly, modern, rounded. Body and UI.
- DM Mono — rates, numbers, tabular data only. Not used decoratively.

**Layout:**
- Rounded-2xl cards with warm borders — tactile, not corporate
- Pill navigation (filled active, outlined inactive) — feels like a product, not a dashboard
- Hero stat card for lowest variable rate — the most important number gets the most space
- Left-aligned, asymmetric layouts — designed, not templated

**Theme:** Light mode primary, dark mode fully supported.

### Anti-Patterns — Never Do These
- No gradient headers or gradient text
- No identical card grids (icon + heading + description repeated)
- No `font-mono` for "data vibes" — only use DM Mono for actual numbers
- No `border-t-4` coloured accent borders
- No `hover:-translate-y` lift shadows
- No glassmorphism or glowing dark mode
- No indigo/violet/purple as primary colours
- No AI slop aesthetics (cyan-on-dark, neon accents, generic hero layouts)
- No jargon in user-facing copy — plain Australian English throughout

### Design Principles
1. **Approachable first, dense second** — The surface should feel welcoming to a first-home buyer. Complexity lives behind filters, drill-downs, and the Analytics tab.
2. **Every number earns its place** — Show rates, comparisons, and trends. Remove anything decorative that doesn't help a user make a decision.
3. **Banks before rates** — Users think in lenders. The Banks view is the default. Rate rows are a secondary lens.
4. **Progressive disclosure** — Best rates visible immediately. Full product details, history, and analytics revealed on demand.
5. **Earned trust through transparency** — Show data source (CDR), freshness timestamp, outlier notes, and the non-advice disclaimer. No hidden agendas.

### Technical Design Constraints
- Tailwind CSS v4 via `@tailwindcss/vite` — no `tailwind.config.js`, tokens defined in `src/index.css` `@theme` block
- All colour tokens use `oklch()` — perceptually uniform, maintainable
- `sand-*` for neutrals, `accent-*` for primary, `sky-*` for fixed rates
- `nums` utility class = DM Mono + tabular-nums — use for all rate/number display
- `rates.db` is latest-snapshot-only (~2.7 MB) — never put history back in the browser DB
- `analytics.json` is pre-computed by the Go aggregator — never run window functions in WASM
- `history.db` lives at repo root, never in `public/` — never served to browsers
